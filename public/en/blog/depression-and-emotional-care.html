Depression - 4 min read

Depression is not weakness. It is a call for care.

Emotional pain, persistent tiredness and withdrawal deserve attention. Seeking help is not a sign of failure.

Depression can affect energy, motivation, sleep, appetite, concentration and the way a person sees themselves and the future. It is not laziness or lack of willpower.

Listening without judgment

Therapy offers a confidential space to understand suffering, observe patterns and build possible steps. The process respects each person's rhythm and may be part of a broader care network.

When to seek support

If sadness, emptiness, hopelessness or loss of interest persists, professional support can help evaluate what is happening and what care is needed.

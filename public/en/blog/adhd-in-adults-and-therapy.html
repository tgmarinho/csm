ADHD in adults: how can therapy help?

For many adults, attention and organization difficulties are accompanied by self-criticism, exhaustion and a feeling of always being late.

Therapy can be a place to understand functioning with less judgment. Instead of reducing the person to symptoms, the process considers history, routine, demands, emotions and relationships.

Beyond productivity

Care may include building realistic strategies for organization, observing avoidance patterns, working with emotional regulation and reducing harsh self-talk.

Individualized support

Each person experiences ADHD differently. Therapy helps translate general information into practical care that respects context and limits.

Therapy for ADHD

Therapy can help teenagers and adults understand attention, organization, impulsivity and self-criticism.

ADHD may appear in daily life as difficulty starting tasks, maintaining focus, regulating emotions, organizing priorities or dealing with frustration. In therapy, these experiences are observed with care, without reducing the person to a diagnosis.

The therapeutic plan depends on each story and context. The goal is to build possible strategies for real life.
